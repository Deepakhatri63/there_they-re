Backlash Counter- Installation

Backlash Counter is an interactive installation that invites the participation of attendees to disrupt the censorship of queer people on social media. Utilising the convening of queer people in nightlife as a resource under the queer commons, we will systematically engage with accounts that have been shadowbanned to work to restore their reach and engagement.

This installation will focus on the accounts of sex workers that have been shadowbanned. Social media accounts are critical to the livelihoods of sex workers helping them advertise and find work in and outside of sex work. However, these accounts are increasingly targetted, restricted, and deleted. This work was inspired by conversations with the Sex Work Section of the FAU Berlin (free workers trade union) and Strippers Collective of Berlin about targeted censorship of queer sex workers and activists.

This is the first in a series of kiosk installations for queer parties that invite attendees to engage with There_They’re art installations and participate in strategic disruptions we hope to scale. If you are interested in hearing more, connecting, or collaborating, please email us.
